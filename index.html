Official Rulebook

All rules are legally binding within the jurisdiction of the Manager’s personal opinion.

Core Rules
  • Gold stars are worth +10 points.
  • Red stars are worth –10 points.
  • One action usually equals one star, unless the behaviour is especially impressive, especially criminal, or requires immediate Manager intervention.
  • Stars are awarded by the System Manager.
  • Tom may appeal a decision, but only with a strong argument, sincere apology, quality snacks, or elite-level banter.
  • Rules are subject to change at the Manager’s discretion.
Gold Star Rules

Initiative and Follow-Through

  • Taking initiative.
  • Following through on something he said he would do.
  • Offering a practical idea, plan, or solution.
  • Remembering a task, plan, or detail.
  • Planning trips.
  • Being reliable in a way that deserves formal recognition.

Snack and Beverage Compliance

  • Remembering my favourite foods.
  • Remembering all intolerances and not treating them as optional side quests.
  • Remembering my coffee order.
  • Arriving with quality snacks.
  • Sharing snacks without being dramatic.
  • Not suggesting food options that are clearly incompatible with my known requirements.

Good Vibes and Banter

  • Delivering a genuinely funny comeback.
  • Sending rateworthy reels.
  • Making me laugh when I am annoyed.
  • Admitting I am funnier.
  • Accepting defeat gracefully when out-bantered.
  • Providing good energy.

Acts of Service

  • Helping with a practical task.
  • Completing approved boy jobs.
  • Offering support.
  • Showing kindness to humans and animals.
  • Using practical skills for the greater good.
  • Cooking.

Approved Boy Jobs

Approved boy jobs include, but are not limited to carrying things, fixing things, opening stubborn jars, moving heavy objects, doing car things, reaching high things, handling practical tasks with minimal complaining, and using strength, height, tools, or general usefulness for the greater good.

Bonus Gold Star Behaviour

Two gold stars may be awarded for exceptional behaviour, including:

  • Showing major emotional intelligence.
  • Agreeing that rap and/or hip-hop is elite.
  • Fixing a mistake without being asked.
  • Planning something genuinely useful, thoughtful, or funny.
  • Performing an act of service so impressive it requires formal recognition.
  • Admitting the Manager was right.
Red Star Rules

Communication Crimes

  • Inconsistent communication without reasonable explanation.
  • Taking excessive time to reply to a simple message when not busy, asleep, working, or otherwise legitimately unavailable.
  • Leaving a message on read with no follow-up.
  • Doubling down instead of owning his actions.
  • Saying “I’ll let you know” and then not letting anyone know.
  • Forgetting important details that have already been formally established.

Planning and Effort Offences

  • Not following through on something he said he would do.
  • Making the Manager organise everything.
  • Forgetting a plan, task, or detail that was already agreed on.
  • Failing to take initiative when initiative was clearly available.
  • Making me walk in the rain.
  • Forgetting my birthday.

Snack and Order Violations

  • Forgetting my order.
  • Bringing bad snacks.
  • Not letting me eat his chips.
  • Refusing to share snacks.
  • Suggesting food that clearly ignores my intolerances.
  • Providing a sweet-to-healthy snack ratio outside the required 20:80 standard.

Vibe and Banter Offences

  • Never telling me I am funny.
  • Refusing to admit I am funnier.
  • Providing poor energy.
  • Sending low-quality reels.
  • Making a bad joke and then doubling down.
  • Failing to appreciate elite banter.

Cultural and Music Crimes

  • Bad music selection.
  • Believing the New Zealand accent is better than the Australian accent.
  • Not liking AFL.
  • Disrespecting rap and/or hip-hop.

Sleep-Related Offences

A red star may be assigned for falling asleep reallyyyyy early if it occurs during active plans, an agreed conversation, or a situation where staying awake was clearly part of the social contract.

Normal sleep does not count as an offence.

No Red Star Zones

Red stars should not be given for:

  • Being asleep.
  • Being at work.
  • Having a genuine emergency.
  • Taking reasonable time to reply when busy.
  • Being honest about needing space or downtime.
  • Things that are actually serious and need a real conversation.
  • Not being available 24/7 like a customer service chatbot.
Redemption Rules

Tom may earn back favour by:

  • Apologising properly.
  • Fixing the issue.
  • Making a better plan.
  • Bringing snacks.
  • Making the Manager laugh.
  • Demonstrating improved compliance over time.
  • Accepting accountability without unnecessary legal defence.
  • Showing improved behaviour across multiple charting periods.

A sincere redemption action may earn a gold star, but it does not automatically remove the original red star. The chart remembers.
